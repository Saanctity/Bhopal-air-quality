Health Impact & Recommendations
👶
Children & Elderly Highly vulnerable. Limit outdoor activities. PM2.5 particles bypass natural defense and enter bloodstream directly.
🫁
Respiratory Risk Frequent wheezing, chest tightness, and persistent cough reported at current AQI levels. Asthma patients at high risk.
🏃
Outdoor Exercise Avoid strenuous outdoor activity. Morning hours (6–8am) show highest PM2.5 concentration — worst time to exercise outside.
🏠
Indoor Protection Keep windows closed. Use air purifiers. Herbal teas and warm water help soothe respiratory tract during high pollution days.
🎆
Festival Warning Diwali fireworks push AQI past 300 (Hazardous). Children under 14, pregnant women most vulnerable during this period.
💊
Medical Conditions Those with pre-existing heart or lung conditions should consult doctors about activity levels during moderate-to-poor AQI days.
